Elec & Me

What to do? Where to start?

These were common questions I had when I was trying to come up with an idea for my thesis project. It is always difficult to narrow down your ideas when there is no direction. What did I do then? I started making a google sheet with a list of my favorite games. Then I listed all of their genres to see what they had in common and what were the differences. That gave me a good idea that I am interested in puzzle and educational games. From there, I started searching for top educational games that are available in the market. I found out there were few games that were not boring, hence very educational.

Those were:

  • Cozmo
  • Osmo
  • Toca Boca

    • All of these had one thing in common: let’s have fun and then you will learn something new inevitably. This gave me the biggest inspiration to use my background in Electrical Engineering and apply that science into entertainment. However, I didn’t know how exactly I can apply that into a game. Therefore, I asked my instructors and mentors at Academy of Art University to give me some clues on what tools are good to be used in order to make a fun educational experience. I started reading Manga stories. There was one story that taught kids elements of Electricity. That was the best inspiration to give me a better idea how I need to approach the basic theories without going into details too much.

    All of those steps helped me to come up with something modern and intuitive to teach kids who already have basic understandings of elementary arithmetics, how some commonly used appliances at home such as lamp, TV, and microwave work. To take advantage of the recent technology, I decided to use Struct Sensor to scan the room in 3D and then it lets the player to place the virtual items in flat surfaces such as a table.

    Despite the fact that this project was initially meant to be created with Struct Sensor and Swift, after Apple introduced ARKit in wwdc and I checked some examples and projects available in Github, I decided to develop my game in ARKit instead.
